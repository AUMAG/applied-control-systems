Concept 2.4: Examples

This is the final concept for this topic. In this concept we go back to Chapter 4 and pull out two key examples to illustrate the concepts covered so far in the course. The first example is a detailed examination of the simulation of a vehicle model with (and without) a cruise control system. Annoyed code is provided so you can interrogate how the various graphs and results are created. The second example is far more brief but illustrates the complexity of a two-degree-of-freedom bicycle model and how the intuitive notion of bicycle stability can arise from a mathematical treatment.
