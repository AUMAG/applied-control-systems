This second module will introduce the three main topics critical to understanding how we implement control systems: state feedback, transfer functions, and frequency domain analysis. Through these topics you will deepen your understanding of how control systems can be used to influence the dynamics of a system and the characteristics you need to consider when doing so.

  Topic 5: Transfer Functions

The next topic in Module II looks at Transfer Functions.

This topic introduces the details of analysing systems in the frequency domain rather than the time domain. This representation also lends itself to the powerful Block Diagram graphical representation of system dynamics and relationships. The frequency domain allows systems to be modelled in terms of Transfer Functions, which are derived using Laplace Transforms and describe the input-output behaviour of a system in terms of magnitude and phase as a function of frequency (plotted visually via the Bode Plot).

The material in this topic is critical to ultimately understanding control systems, and has the largest number of concepts of all the topics in the course.
