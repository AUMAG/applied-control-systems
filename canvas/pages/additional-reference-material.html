Essentials of Engineering Mathematics (Jeffrey 2004)

This textbook outlines the full breadth of applied mathematics topics that we typically cover across a general engineering degree. (Not all topics will be relevant to all disciplines, and nowadays some of the theoretical mathematical material is overlooked in favour of computation approaches.)

In particular for this course, Essentials of Engineering Mathematics contains some good (simple) examples of ODEs for a range of engineering problems (§69) and concise but approachable reference material on ODE solutions, both analytic (§70) and numeric (§71).

Standard solutions are covered for second-order ODEs (§79) in terms of free and forced oscillations and how damping ratios affect the solutions, followed by detailed sections covering Laplace Transforms and their application to solving differential equations. (§81–§86)
