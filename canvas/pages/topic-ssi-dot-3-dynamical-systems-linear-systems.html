  Topic 3: Linear Systems

The third and final topic in this module looks at Linear Systems, a certain subset of the types of dynamical system.

At a certain level of fidelity, all physical systems contain nonlinearities. However, even weak nonlinearities can make ODEs intractable and depending on the form of the nonlinearity require individual treatment for an uncountable number of classes of dynamic system. Luckily, almost all engineering systems can be approximated with linear models around their operating point, and this topic details this process in sufficient detail that:

  • you will be able to look up how to do it in the future if the need arises, and
  • you will have some level of confidence that linear models are appropriate in many circumstances.

After reviewing the concepts above and completing the activities, you should now:

  • Understand the process of identifying an equilibrium and linearising a system around this operating point

  Module I conclusion

The first module of this course has introduced

  • the modelling of dynamical systems
  • their general behaviour, and finally
  • the linearisation and solution types of these systems to make them tractable for control systems analysis.

Along the way we have provided indications of

  • how these are linked with control systems, and
  • the types of problems that we look to solve using control.

In the next module, we will introduce more formally the fundamental concepts of control theory, particularly related to how we represent feedback control systems and analyse their properties.
