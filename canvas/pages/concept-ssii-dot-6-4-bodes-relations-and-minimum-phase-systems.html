Concept 6.4: Bode’s Relations and Minimum Phase Systems

A minimum phase system is one which follows the minimum amount of phase change given the visible changes of gain (e.g., in a Bode Plot). Non-minimum phase systems have particular dynamics (time delays; zeros or poles with positive real components) that make the system harder to control. This concept discusses these relationships.
