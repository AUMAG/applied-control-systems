Module I: Dynamical Systems  (Topics 1–3)

Over three topics below, this module will generalise concepts from Engineering Modelling 1, Engineering Mechanics and Dynamics into the study of Dynamical Systems.

  • In the first topic (below), we introduce the course and the concepts of modelling dynamical systems, including basic control systems.
  • The second topic covers Ordinary Differential Equations with a focus on characterising their behaviour, in particular their stability.
  • The third and final topic in this module looks at linear systems. To make the control of dynamical systems tractable we must restrict our scope to linear systems, or rather a linearised approximation around the operating position of a given dynamical system that we wish to control.

  Topic 1: Introduction and System Modelling

This topic will introduce you to control systems concepts and system modelling. One of the keys to success in control systems is to recognise that it is built upon the foundation of Dynamical Systems. Once our dynamical system is modelled with a standardised methodology, we can then approach control systems using a suite of sophisticated tools.

  Topic 1 conclusion

After reviewing the concepts above and completing the activities, you should now:

  • Understand conceptually what a control system is and does
  • Understand the terminology of control systems, in particular: system inputs and outputs, state variables, state space systems, linear time invariant systems, and how these terms are linked together via ordinary differential equations to model dynamical systems
  • Identify state variables within a differential equation and how these are used in both linear and nonlinear models of dynamical systems
  • Extract key parameters from time domain graphs of simple system models: Integrator Time Delay (ITD) model, First Order Time Delay model (FOTD), and Second Order Time Delay model (SOTD) 
  • Be able to create ODE models in Matlab and Simulink and simulate them with inputs such as a Step Response
