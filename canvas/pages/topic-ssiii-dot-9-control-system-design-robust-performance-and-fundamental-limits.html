  Topic 9: Robust Performance & Fundamental Limits

In the final topic of the course, we combine elements of several previous topics by analysing the performance of robust controllers due to various factors, in particular measurement noise and external disturbances. These analyses allow us to draw conclusions on the fundamental limitations of linear control systems, and indicate directions of improvement that may be possible by considering various types of nonlinear control systems.

  Topic 9 conclusion

After reviewing the concepts above and completing the activities, you should now:

  • represent uncertainty in a block diagram / transfer function model of a control system;
  • quantitatively analyse the effects of uncertainty on the stability and performance of a control system;
  • consider the trade-offs in system design when it comes to robustness, performance, and rejection of noise/disturbances;
  • consider pole placement in a more general way;
  • ensure that real-world effects such as saturation, rate limits, hysteresis, quantisation noise, and such nonlinear phenomena are included in your simulation models and are considered in the control system design process.

  Module III conclusion

The third module of this course has introduced:

  • the common PID Controller in detail, including concepts of integrator wind-up and implementation details;
  • the foundation for a control system design process that considers performance in the context of reference tracking, measurement noise and disturbance rejection; and,
  • how to model parameter uncertainty, nonlinearities, and analyse the effects of uncertainty on the stability and performance of a control system in the context of a robust control system framework.

These topics lead directly into more advanced topics such as the Kalman Filter, Optimal Control, H-Infinity Control, and multi-variable control systems in general.
