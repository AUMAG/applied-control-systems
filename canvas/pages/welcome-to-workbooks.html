Workbooks

 

This is a course which requires active engagement with the textbook, slide material, computer exercises, and practicals. Several of these activities require you to submit a non-graded workbook as part of the activity. What do we mean by a workbook?

  • A workbook should be an individual record of any activity you undertake
  • Each record must contain a time and date (which can be important in industry when considering various Intellectual Property matters) 
  • Each record should contain working, commentary, corrections, and conclusions about an activity
  • We are not looking for you to write an essay about what you are doing!
  • Merely that you are keeping notes, and you should be able to use these notes later in the course to assist

For instance, in the computer workshops you will be completing various Matlab/Simulink exercises. The skills developed at these sessions will be used directly in the assignments linked with each module. The earlier you keep your work organised, the easier you will find drawing together the different facets of the course

 

Digital tools

We do not mandate the use of a specific tool in this course. It can be as simple as a Microsoft Word or OneNote document which you copy/paste material into as you go.

You may wish to consider the digital tool LabArchives. It allows a more official record of your work to be stored, with useful hierarchical structures in keeping material organised. It uses automatic timestamps, and entries/collections can be shared with collaborators and edited somewhat collaboratively if working in a group.
